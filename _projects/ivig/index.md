## Purpose:
Reduction of immunosuppression can lead to poor clinical outcomes such as increased rejection, development of donor specific antibodies (DSA), diminished allograft function, and increased risk of allograft loss. Immunosuppression reduction can be categorized by either patient initiated (medication nonadherence) versus transplant team driven due to PTLD, infections or viremias. Short courses of intravenous immunoglobulin (IVIG) have been utilized to either treat viremia, prevent/treat DSA, or do both. The use of IVIG for maintenance immunosuppression is scarce. We aimed to examine the longitudinal effects of maintenance IVIG on clinical outcomes in pediatric kidney transplant recipients with and without medication nonadherence.

## Methods:
We performed a retrospective longitudinal observational study of 40 pediatric kidney transplant recipients receiving maintenance IVIG. Patients were either induced with corticosteroids or antithymocyte globulin and maintained on tacrolimus, steroids, and mycophenolate mofetil (MMF). For physician initiated immunosuppression reduction MMF was either held or reduced and tacrolimus goals lowered to 3-6. Patient driven medication reduction was determined by self-reported nonadherence and also verified by undetectable tacrolimus or mycophenolic acid (MPA) levels. We compared estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR ≥ 30 vs < 30 mL/min/1.73 m2) as calculated by the Under 25 Chronic Kidney Disease (U25 CKiD) equation and allograft loss defined as return to dialysis using Fisher’s exact test in adherent versus nonadherent recipients.

## Results:
{% include image-gallery.html images="results.png" height="400" %} 
Graft loss was observed in 8 of 40 recipients (17.5%). A greater proportion of graft loss was observed among non-adherent patients compared with adherent patients (15% vs 2.5%, p = 0.007). Recipients with eGFR < 30 experienced a higher frequency of graft loss than those with eGFR ≥ 30 (17.5% vs 0%, p = 0.00001). Adherent patients treated with IVIG had better kidney function compared to nonadherent patients. 60%  of adherent patients had an eGFR of >30 compared to 22.5% of nonadherent recipients (p=0.0002).

## Conclusion:
IVIG maintenance therapy for adherent patients is associated with better kidney function and lower rates of allograft failure compared to its use in recipients with nonadherence. These findings support the importance of careful patient selection for long-term IVIG and highlight the need for larger prospective studies.
